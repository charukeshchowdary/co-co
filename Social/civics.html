Class 6 Social Science

Introduction to Civics

Civics is the study of the rights and duties of citizenship. It helps us understand how governments work, the roles of various institutions, and the importance of participating in community life. In Class 6, civics usually covers basic concepts about governance, democracy, and community responsibilities.

Understanding Democracy

Definition: Democracy is a form of government where the power is vested in the people. Citizens have the right to vote and elect their leaders.

Features: Free and fair elections, rule of law, and protection of individual rights.

The Role of Government

Functions: The government is responsible for making laws, enforcing them, and providing public services. It also manages resources and ensures justice.

Types: Understanding different levels of government - local, state, and national.

Rights and Responsibilities of Citizens

Rights: Fundamental rights include freedom of speech, the right to equality, and the right to education.

Responsibilities: Duties such as obeying laws, voting, and participating in community activities.

The Importance of Rules and Laws

Purpose: Rules and laws help maintain order and ensure that everyone is treated fairly.

Examples: School rules, traffic laws, and community regulations.

Community and Citizenship

Community: A group of people living in the same area with shared interests and goals.

Active Citizenship: Involves participating in community service, respecting others, and contributing to the well-being of society.
